# Project Dead Drop Lore

## Setting

Project Dead Drop takes place inside the Star Citizen universe. It is a fan-made ARG story built around regeneration technology, hidden trade routes, Banu grey-market dealing, Vanduul hostility, and the darker places where human desperation becomes something that can be bought.

The ARG begins with a message.

The player receives a link to the login website and two words:

```text
find me
```

At first, the sender is unknown. It could be a surviving fragment of the missing person, a delayed recovery signal, a trap, or someone inside the network trying to expose what happened. The uncertainty is part of the hook. The message pulls the player toward a system they were never meant to access.

Behind that message is a personal search. Someone the player loved did not come back after regeneration. Their body was gone, the process should have completed, and the records claim enough to look official at a glance. But the final imprint never arrived where it was supposed to.

The case file calls it 07-LOSTREGEN.

## Core Premise

A loved person's regeneration did not complete because their regen imprint was stolen.

At first, the disappearance looks like a clinic failure, a corrupted ledger, or a bad transition checksum. The deeper trail points somewhere worse: an underground network moving stolen human imprint data through grey-market cargo channels.

The network is built around Banu grey-market dealers who will trade anything if the profit is high enough. To them, a human imprint is not sacred. It is a rare commodity, a data package with desperate buyers, dangerous uses, and almost no clean paper trail.

Behind the dealers is a second interest: the Vanduul. They want access to human regeneration and imprint data so they can study how humans survive, recover, remember, and return to battle. The goal is not compassion or science for its own sake. It is advantage. If they can understand the human mind and the mechanics of regeneration, they can learn how to break both more effectively.

## The Emotional Hook

The player is not chasing an abstract stolen file. They are chasing the last remaining trace of someone who mattered.

Every command, manifest, relay ping, and decoded payload is haunted by the first message:

```text
find me
```

That message becomes the emotional thread through the investigation. The player does not know who sent it at the beginning, but the sender's identity should become clearer as the ARG progresses.

The horror of the ARG comes from realizing that the missing person may not simply be dead. Their imprint may have been copied, altered, tested, sold, or used as experimental material.

The player begins with grief and ends up inside a conspiracy.

## Current ARG Flow

The current storyline begins when the player receives the login website and the message:

```text
find me
```

The login page is the first puzzle gate. The player uses the clues on the page to authenticate into the recovery console. At this stage, they do not know who sent the message or whether the system is safe.

Inside the console, the player follows recovered depot records, command outputs, encoded payloads, and route fragments. Solving this first chain points them toward the first physical investigation site:

```text
L4 Shallow Fields Station
```

At L4, the player finds a code hidden near a grey-market terminal. The terminal is used to send concealed messages and trade illegal commodities. This discovery confirms that the missing regen imprint was not lost by accident. It passed through a criminal communications and trade system.

The L4 code should become the bridge into the next phase of the ARG. It can unlock another page, expose a deeper terminal command, identify a Banu broker, or point to the first evidence of Vanduul-linked experimentation.

## The Network

The dead drop network hides stolen regen data inside ordinary cargo traffic. Legal and semi-legal goods move along known routes while the real payload travels in the data channel. A clean manifest can hide a dirty transfer.

The current route appears to be:

```text
clinic -> carrier -> grey-market relay -> Banu broker -> Vanduul research channel
```

The Banu dealers are not necessarily loyal to the Vanduul. They are loyal to profit. Their role is to acquire, launder, split, and move forbidden data through trade systems that already operate at the edge of law and oversight.

The Vanduul interest gives the crime its larger threat. Stolen imprints are not only being sold. They are being studied.

## The Horror Beneath The Case

As the ARG progresses, the player should uncover signs of hidden and horrible experiments conducted by both humans and Vanduul.

Human actors may be involved because greed, fear, military ambition, or black-budget research made them willing to cross the line first. They may have built the tools, opened access to clinic systems, or supplied victims through corrupted recovery infrastructure.

The Vanduul side turns the stolen data into a weaponized study of humanity. Regen imprints could help them test memory response, trauma loops, identity degradation, combat behavior, fear conditioning, or the limits of human continuity.

The horror is not only that someone was stolen. It is that the system built to bring people back can become a supply chain.

## Known Trail

The clinic record confirms that a regeneration receipt exists, but the completion event is missing. Transition residue remains, which means the process began and something interrupted or redirected the imprint.

One identifier, ID-413, appears across multiple ledgers. It validates parts of the goods manifest, but it also ties suspicious relay traffic to the same movement chain. The goods may have been legitimate. The hidden payload was not.

Known signs:

- A regeneration receipt exists.
- The final completion event is missing.
- The clinic checksum does not match the transition record.
- The goods ledger appears clean.
- The data channel carries extra weight.
- Banu trade traffic overlaps with the final imprint ping.
- An illegal device appears in the route metadata.
- Vanduul-linked research traffic is suspected beyond the first marker.

## The Depots

### Depot 01: Grey-Market Goods Manifest

Depot 01 contains cargo records. These entries look ordinary on the surface: aluminum, steel, iron, plastic, water, and med-gel moving between stations and systems.

The important clue is that the manifest is clean while the carrier note warns that the data channel must be reviewed separately. This establishes the central trick of the case: cargo is being used as camouflage for stolen regeneration data.

### Depot 02: Relay Data And Message Pings

Depot 02 is the key evidence cache. It records pings, messages, packages, acknowledgements, and bursts moving through the relay network.

One package contains a base64 payload:

```text
TDQgU2hhbGxvdyBGaWVsZHMgU3RhdGlvbg==
```

Decoding it reveals the current location marker:

```text
L4 Shallow Fields Station
```

This is the first major recovery point in the case.

### Depot 03: Banu Exchange Fragments

Depot 03 contains incomplete Banu exchange records. The translation confidence is too low for full recovery, but the fragments confirm that trade traffic overlaps with the suspicious imprint trail.

The Banu exchange is likely not the final destination. It is a market layer: noisy enough to hide a signal, flexible enough to move forbidden data, and profitable enough that someone would look away.

## L4 Shallow Fields Station

L4 Shallow Fields Station is the first recovered location marker. It is described as a quiet transfer point outside normal route checks, a place where grey-market cargo, relay pings, and the missing imprint all appear to intersect.

The riddle describes the location as:

```text
I am not a station, though cargo waits in my dark.
I am not a planet, though two bodies teach me where to stand.
I am the fourth quiet knot in a five-point crown.
Smugglers love my stillness because patrols look past the balance.
```

This points to the L4 Lagrange region: a stable orbital balance point useful for hiding low-power devices, relay markers, unregistered transfers, and dead drops.

L4 is not the end of the story. It is where the player proves the missing regen was moved deliberately.

## The Illegal Device

Trace data suggests that an illegal device appears in the route metadata. It did not move the imprint by itself. Instead, it tagged the transition record and handed it through the trade lane.

The device signature is intermittent, low-power, and masked by trade-band traffic. That makes it difficult to detect unless the investigator connects the cargo records, relay pings, encoded payloads, and Banu exchange fragments.

This device may be human-built, Banu-modified, or Vanduul-repurposed. The uncertainty should be part of the tension.

## Antagonist Layers

### Banu Grey-Market Dealers

The Banu dealers are the visible criminal layer. They move the stolen imprint data because it is valuable. They may not care who bought it, what it will be used for, or whether the victim can ever return.

They are dangerous because they make the crime mundane. To them, the missing person is inventory.

### Human Collaborators

Some humans may be complicit. They could include clinic insiders, data brokers, smugglers, private security, researchers, or officials who buried failed experiments.

Their motive can vary from money to ambition to fear. Their presence makes the conspiracy more horrifying because the victim was not only taken by an outside enemy. They were betrayed from within human space.

### Vanduul Interests

The Vanduul are the deepest threat. They want to understand humans well enough to fight them better.

Stolen regen data could help them study:

- Human memory continuity.
- Psychological resilience after trauma.
- Combat recovery patterns.
- Identity degradation.
- Fear response and conditioning.
- Weak points in regeneration infrastructure.

The player should gradually realize that the missing loved one may be one subject among many.

## Current Case Status

The subject remains unresolved. The imprint is in transit, copied, or displaced. The location marker has been recovered, but the broker identity and final destination are still unknown.

Current known marker:

```text
L4 Shallow Fields Station
```

Current suspected chain:

```text
stolen regen imprint -> Banu grey-market relay -> Vanduul experimental pipeline
```

## Themes

Project Dead Drop is about grief, exploitation, and the terror of becoming data in a universe that treats data as cargo.

The central question is not only where the missing imprint went, but what was done to it after it was stolen.

The ARG should feel like a descent:

- From personal loss into criminal trade.
- From criminal trade into human corruption.
- From human corruption into Vanduul experimentation.
- From one missing loved one into evidence of a much larger atrocity.

The player's role is to follow the residue, decode the handoffs, expose the network, and find out whether anything of the person they loved can still be recovered.
