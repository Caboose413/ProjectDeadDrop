# Project Dead Drop Lore

## Core Premise

Project Dead Drop follows Case 07-LOSTREGEN, an investigation into a missing regeneration imprint. A transition should have completed cleanly through a clinic ledger, but the final completion event is absent. What remains is transition residue, a sign that the subject's imprint may have been intercepted, displaced, or deliberately routed through another system.

The recovery node is not a public terminal. It is a provisional access point for tracing a concealed data handoff hidden inside ordinary trade traffic.

## The Mystery

The case begins with a simple contradiction: the goods ledger is clean, but the data channel is not.

Cargo manifests show routine movement through grey-market routes, yet several records carry suspicious metadata. Message pings overlap with trade activity. Payload weights do not match declarations. A transition checksum fails to line up with the clinic ledger. Together, these clues suggest that someone used regular cargo movement as cover for a hidden imprint transfer.

The dead drop is therefore not a box, a meeting, or a physical exchange. It is a handoff buried inside normal traffic, where data can move without attracting attention.

## Known Trail

The current route appears to be:

```text
clinic -> carrier -> grey-market relay -> unknown
```

The clinic record confirms that a regeneration receipt exists, but the completion event is missing. From there, the trail passes through a carrier and into grey-market relay traffic. The final destination is unresolved at the start of the investigation.

One identifier, ID-413, appears across multiple ledgers. It validates the goods manifest, but it also ties the suspicious data channel to the same movement chain. The goods may have been legitimate. The hidden payload was not.

## The Depots

### Depot 01: Grey-Market Goods Manifest

Depot 01 contains cargo records. These entries look ordinary on the surface: aluminum, steel, iron, plastic, water, and med-gel moving between stations and systems.

The important clue is that the manifest is clean while the carrier note warns that the data channel must be reviewed separately. This establishes the central trick of the case: legal or semi-legal cargo is being used as camouflage for something else.

### Depot 02: Relay Data And Message Pings

Depot 02 is the key evidence cache. It records pings, messages, packages, acknowledgements, and bursts moving through the relay network.

One package contains a base64 payload:

```text
TDQgU2hhbGxvdyBGaWVsZHMgU3RhdGlvbg==
```

Decoding it reveals the current location marker:

```text
L4 Shallow Fields Station
```

This is the first major recovery point in the case.

### Depot 03: Banu Exchange Fragments

Depot 03 contains incomplete Banu exchange records. The translation confidence is too low for full recovery, but the fragments confirm that trade traffic overlaps with the suspicious imprint trail.

The Banu exchange may not be the origin of the crime. It may be part of the masking layer: noisy enough to hide a signal, ordinary enough to avoid immediate attention.

## L4 Shallow Fields Station

L4 Shallow Fields Station is the recovered location marker. It is described as a quiet transfer point outside normal route checks, a place where grey-market cargo, relay pings, and the missing imprint all appear to intersect.

The riddle describes the location as:

```text
I am not a station, though cargo waits in my dark.
I am not a planet, though two bodies teach me where to stand.
I am the fourth quiet knot in a five-point crown.
Smugglers love my stillness because patrols look past the balance.
```

This points to the L4 Lagrange region: a stable orbital balance point often useful for hiding low-power devices, relay markers, or unregistered transfers.

## The Illegal Device

Trace data suggests that an illegal device appears in the route metadata. It did not move the imprint by itself. Instead, it tagged the transition record and handed it through the trade lane.

The device signature is intermittent, low-power, and masked by trade-band traffic. That makes it difficult to detect unless the investigator connects the cargo records, relay pings, and encoded payload.

## Current Case Status

The subject remains unresolved. The imprint is in transit or has been displaced. The location marker has been recovered, but the broker identity is still unknown.

Known facts:

- A regeneration receipt exists.
- The completion event is missing.
- The clinic checksum does not match the transition record.
- Banu trade traffic overlaps with the final imprint ping.
- ID-413 appears across the relevant ledgers.
- L4 Shallow Fields Station is the current recovered marker.
- An extraction attempt is suspected.

## Themes

Project Dead Drop is built around hidden movement, partial recovery, and the unease of identity becoming cargo.

The central question is not only where the missing imprint went, but what it became while in transit. The payload is described as behaving like memory, which suggests the case may involve more than stolen data. It may involve a person reduced to a transferable signal.

The player's role is to follow the residue, decode the handoff, and determine who used the dead drop to move a human imprint through the machinery of trade.
